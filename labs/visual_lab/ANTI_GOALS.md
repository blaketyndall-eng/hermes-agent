# Visual Lab Anti-Goals

These are behaviors and directions the lab should actively avoid.

The goal is believable image behavior, not aesthetic imitation.

---

## Do not build fake nostalgia

Avoid:

- fake film borders
- sprocket overlays
- fake timestamps
- fake disposable-camera frames
- VHS overlays
- fake dust and scratches
- fake projector artifacts
- gimmick degradation

---

## Do not destroy images for style

Avoid:

- crushed blacks with no texture
- exaggerated bloom
- extreme grain
- unusable blur
- fake chromatic aberration
- excessive color shifts
- fake lens dirt
- aggressive JPEG destruction

---

## Do not imitate social-media presets

Avoid:

- one-click vintage looks
- trendy filter aesthetics
- over-stylized color grading
- cinematic LUT thinking
- hyper-orange teal balance
- exaggerated halation

---

## Do not optimize for obviousness

The best TRACE behavior should:

- feel subtle
- feel believable
- feel difficult to identify directly
- feel emotionally present rather than visually loud

Users should feel the image before noticing the processing.

---

## Do not confuse technical failure with realism

Some imperfection is useful.

Random destruction is not.

Every visual behavior should support:

- atmosphere
- memory realism
- physical light behavior
- emotional believability

---

## Do not over-automate taste

Human review should remain central.

The lab should support:

- comparison
- reflection
- iteration
- experiment lineage

Not fully automated aesthetic decisions.
