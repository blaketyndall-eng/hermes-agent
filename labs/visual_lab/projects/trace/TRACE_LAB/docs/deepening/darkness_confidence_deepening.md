# TRACE Deepening: Darkness Confidence

Darkness is not a single behavior.

TRACE should avoid treating darkness as merely:

```text
less exposure
```

Darkness can carry different emotional, spatial, and perceptual functions.

---

# Core Thesis

Modern phone cameras often treat darkness as a defect to repair.

TRACE should treat darkness as visual information.

Not all darkness should be lifted.
Not all darkness should be crushed.

The key question is:

```text
What kind of darkness is this?
```

---

# Darkness Types

## Dimensional Darkness

Darkness that still feels physical and layered.

Useful when:

- shadows have texture
- surfaces remain barely readable
- space feels present but hidden

Failure mode:

- muddy black collapse

---

## Environmental Disappearance

Darkness that causes the room or background to fall away.

Useful when:

- foreground pressure matters
- direct flash isolates subjects
- memory pressure depends on incomplete space

Failure mode:

- background becomes fake or overly stylized

---

## Protective Darkness

Darkness that gives an image privacy or emotional shelter.

Useful when:

- the image feels intimate
- the scene is ordinary or domestic
- not everything should be exposed

Failure mode:

- image becomes too vague or evasive

---

## Hostile Darkness

Darkness that creates threat, uncertainty, or tension.

Useful when:

- parking lots
- empty streets
- motel exteriors
- unfamiliar interiors

Failure mode:

- melodrama or artificial menace

---

## Empty Darkness

Darkness that suggests absence rather than depth.

Useful when:

- loneliness matters
- negative space carries emotion
- the image feels late, quiet, or depleted

Failure mode:

- dead frame with no anchor

---

## Ritual Darkness

Darkness repeated across a sequence until it becomes part of the roll’s identity.

Useful when:

- a roll needs coherence
- recurrence matters
- late-night perception accumulates

Failure mode:

- repetitive darkness that feels like a preset

---

# Review Questions

When reviewing dark images, ask:

- Is darkness preserving atmosphere or destroying information?
- Does darkness feel physical?
- Does darkness create privacy, threat, emptiness, or disappearance?
- Is the image more believable because something remains hidden?
- Would a phone have made this worse by revealing too much?
