# TRACE Deepening: Hallway Perception

Hallways are psychologically powerful TRACE environments.

They often create:

```text
repetitive spatial perception
```

under emotionally unstable conditions.

---

# Core Thesis

Hallways compress perception.

They produce:

- architectural repetition
- directional pressure
- spatial fatigue
- depth uncertainty
- environmental numbness
- recurring light behavior

This makes hallways emotionally important despite their ordinary appearance.

---

# Hallway Modes

## Corridor Fatigue

Repeated hallway exposure weakens perceptual clarity.

Examples:

- repeated doors
- recurring wall texture
- repeated overhead lights

Emotional effect:

- exhaustion
- temporal blur
- environmental flattening

---

## Directional Pressure

The environment forces visual movement.

Examples:

- narrow framing
- tunnel-like perspective
- compressed architecture

Emotional effect:

- claustrophobia
- anticipation
- instability

---

## Architectural Repetition

The environment feels emotionally repetitive.

Examples:

- repeating doors
- repeated room spacing
- identical lighting intervals

Emotional effect:

- ritual feeling
- environmental recurrence
- perceptual monotony

---

## Threshold Perception

The hallway acts as a transition between emotional states.

Examples:

- entering a room
- leaving a gathering
- motel walkway movement

Emotional effect:

- uncertainty
- anticipation
- emotional drift

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not exaggerate hallway tension cinematically.

It should preserve:

```text
ordinary perceptual compression
```

---

# Review Questions

- Did the hallway feel repetitive?
- Did architecture flatten naturally?
- Did the environment create directional pressure?
- Did threshold movement feel believable?
- Would a phone have over-corrected spatial uncertainty?
