# TRACE Deepening: Peripheral People

Peripheral people are often emotionally important despite incomplete visibility.

Phones tend to prioritize:

- primary faces
- centered subjects
- semantic clarity

TRACE should increasingly preserve:

```text
emotionally meaningful peripheral presence
```

---

# Core Thesis

Many believable memories contain:

- people at frame edges
- half-visible bodies
- background figures
- hallway silhouettes
- mirror reflections
- detached observers

These people often create:

- social realism
- atmosphere
- memory pressure
- observational authenticity

without becoming the main subject.

---

# Peripheral Presence Modes

## Edge Presence

A person partially enters or exits the frame.

Examples:

- cropped shoulder
- half-visible arm
- detached silhouette

Emotional effect:

- accidental realism
- observational honesty
- environmental continuity

---

## Background Witness

A distant or secondary figure subtly alters the emotional reading.

Examples:

- hallway observer
- distant crowd member
- seated background figure

Emotional effect:

- social depth
- environmental realism
- subconscious tension

---

## Reflection Presence

A person appears indirectly through reflective surfaces.

Examples:

- mirror flash figure
- window reflection
- metallic surface distortion

Emotional effect:

- perceptual layering
- fragmented social perception
- memory complexity

---

## Detached Presence

A figure feels emotionally disconnected from the main scene.

Examples:

- isolated standing body
- disconnected eye contact
- separate environmental position

Emotional effect:

- loneliness
- nightlife realism
- emotional distance

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not force peripheral emphasis.

It should preserve:

```text
naturally incomplete social awareness
```

---

# Review Questions

- Did peripheral people contribute emotionally?
- Did the environment feel socially populated?
- Did the frame preserve accidental realism?
- Did secondary figures remain subtle?
- Would a phone have over-prioritized the main subject?
