# TRACE Deepening: Social Ambiguity

TRACE increasingly studies:

```text
social information that remains emotionally believable without becoming fully resolved
```

---

# Core Thesis

Real social perception is often incomplete.

Memory rarely preserves:

- exact expressions
- complete body language
- fully readable interaction
- perfect eye contact

Instead, memory often preserves:

- uncertain gestures
- partial attention
- ambiguous interaction
- emotionally unresolved moments

Phones aggressively reduce this ambiguity.

TRACE should increasingly preserve it.

---

# Social Ambiguity Modes

## Uncertain Attention

It is unclear where attention is directed.

Examples:

- off-frame gaze
- partial eye visibility
- detached staring

Emotional effect:

- realism
- vulnerability
- observational tension

---

## Ambiguous Interaction

The relationship between people is unresolved.

Examples:

- unclear touch
- interrupted conversation
- uncertain body positioning

Emotional effect:

- social realism
- emotional openness
- memory authenticity

---

## Partial Expression

Emotion is only partially visible.

Examples:

- half-lit faces
- blurred reactions
- obscured expressions

Emotional effect:

- emotional complexity
- realism
- perceptual honesty

---

## Peripheral Emotion

Emotion exists outside the main subject.

Examples:

- distant observer
- edge-frame reaction
- mirror reflection face

Emotional effect:

- environmental realism
- layered social feeling
- memory depth

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not intentionally obscure emotion.

It should preserve:

```text
naturally unresolved social perception
```

---

# Review Questions

- Did the interaction remain believable?
- Was the emotional information over-resolved?
- Did ambiguity strengthen realism?
- Did peripheral social information matter emotionally?
- Would a phone have made the moment feel too explicit?
