# TRACE Deepening: Social Perception

TRACE increasingly studies how people are perceived under imperfect visual conditions.

Not as clean portraits.
Not as optimized faces.
Not as fully resolved subjects.

But as:

```text
partial social information
```

inside unstable environments.

---

# Core Thesis

Emotionally believable images often preserve people incompletely.

Modern phones tend to optimize:

- face clarity
- skin correction
- eye visibility
- subject separation
- expression legibility
- social readability

TRACE should increasingly preserve:

- partial social visibility
- ambiguous interaction
- social distance
- crowd detachment
- peripheral bodies
- incomplete gestures

---

# Social Perception Modes

## Social Ambiguity

The viewer cannot fully resolve the social relationship or intention.

Examples:

- uncertain eye contact
- unclear gesture
- half-visible expression
- ambiguous body language

Emotional effect:

- realism
- observational tension
- memory authenticity

---

## Crowd Detachment

People appear socially close but emotionally disconnected.

Examples:

- floating faces in darkness
- isolated bodies inside a crowd
- detached group behavior

Emotional effect:

- nightlife realism
- social fatigue
- emotional distance

---

## Peripheral Presence

People outside the main subject area carry emotional weight.

Examples:

- cropped body at frame edge
- hallway figure
- background friend
- mirror reflection person

Emotional effect:

- accidental realism
- social proof
- memory pressure

---

## Observer Distance

The camera feels socially positioned rather than objectively neutral.

Examples:

- outsider perspective
- too-close flash
- uncertain participation
- voyeuristic distance

Emotional effect:

- social realism
- vulnerability
- participation ambiguity

---

## Social Fatigue

People appear emotionally depleted or performatively tired.

Examples:

- weakened poses
- late expressions
- repeated gestures
- disconnected attention

Emotional effect:

- temporal realism
- nightlife exhaustion
- social haze

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not make people look worse.

It should preserve:

```text
believable social incompleteness
```

That is very different from:

- ugly filters
- artificial degradation
- face distortion
- stylized candidness

---

# Review Questions

- Is the social information fully resolved or partially withheld?
- Does ambiguity improve believability?
- Do peripheral people strengthen memory pressure?
- Does the camera feel socially situated?
- Would a phone have over-clarified the human moment?
