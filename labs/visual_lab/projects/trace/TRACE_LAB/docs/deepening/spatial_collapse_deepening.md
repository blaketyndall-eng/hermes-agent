# TRACE Deepening: Spatial Collapse

Spatial collapse is not simply:

```text
shallow depth
```

It is a perceptual condition where space becomes:

- incomplete
- detached
- swallowed
- isolated
- emotionally unstable

---

# Core Thesis

Many emotionally believable direct-flash images contain:

- detached foregrounds
- disappearing backgrounds
- swallowed corners
- isolated figures
- incomplete architecture

Phones aggressively normalize this.

TRACE should increasingly preserve:

```text
believable incomplete space
```

---

# Spatial Collapse Modes

## Claustrophobic Collapse

Space feels compressed and physically close.

Examples:

- bathroom flash
- crowded interiors
- backseat photos

Emotional effect:

- tension
- intimacy
- overstimulation

---

## Empty Collapse

Space disappears into absence.

Examples:

- empty parking lots
- black suburban streets
- late sidewalks

Emotional effect:

- loneliness
- detachment
- memory drift

---

## Social Collapse

People detach from environment.

Examples:

- floating faces under flash
- isolated bodies in darkness
- disconnected groups

Emotional effect:

- nightlife realism
- social instability
- awkwardness

---

## Architectural Collapse

Rooms become spatially incomplete.

Examples:

- swallowed ceilings
- black hallways
- fragmented interiors

Emotional effect:

- perceptual uncertainty
- instability
- environmental ambiguity

---

## Distance Collapse

Foreground and background lose normal separation.

Examples:

- flash-heavy streets
- compressed perspective
- reflective surfaces flattening depth

Emotional effect:

- surveillance feeling
- memory compression
- emotional immediacy

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not create fake cinematic depth.

It should preserve:

```text
physically believable incomplete visibility
```

---

# Review Questions

- Does the image feel spatially incomplete?
- Does space feel physically believable?
- Did foreground pressure isolate the subject?
- Did the environment collapse naturally?
- Would a phone have flattened this tension?
