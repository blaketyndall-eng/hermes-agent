# TRACE Deepening: Transitional Environments

TRACE increasingly studies spaces people pass through rather than spaces people fully inhabit.

These spaces are emotionally important because they create:

```text
temporary perception
```

People are often:

- arriving
- leaving
- waiting
- drifting
- pacing
- recovering
- separating
- transitioning

This produces unstable visual and social behavior.

---

# Core Thesis

Emotionally believable memory often forms in transitional spaces.

Examples:

- parking lots
- hallways
- stairwells
- gas stations
- sidewalks
- motel walkways
- convenience stores
- elevators
- apartment breezeways
- back alleys
- drive-thrus

These environments tend to produce:

- partial visibility
- weak social performance
- spatial collapse
- repeated architecture
- environmental emptiness
- detached observation
- lighting instability

---

# Transitional Environment Modes

## Arrival Space

The environment marks entry into an experience.

Examples:

- parking lot before a bar
- motel exterior
- apartment breezeway

Emotional effect:

- anticipation
- uncertainty
- social adjustment

---

## Exit Space

The environment marks departure or emotional after-effect.

Examples:

- sidewalk after party
- convenience store after midnight
- parking lot after leaving

Emotional effect:

- depletion
- aftermath
- loneliness

---

## Waiting Space

Nothing dramatic happens, but perception becomes emotionally loaded.

Examples:

- gas station pump
- lobby
- hallway
- drive-thru

Emotional effect:

- detachment
- anticipation
- environmental numbness

---

## Drift Space

The subject is moving without strong destination feeling.

Examples:

- sidewalk walk
- motel walkway
- empty street

Emotional effect:

- memory blur
- spatial uncertainty
- social detachment

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not romanticize transitional spaces.

It should preserve:

```text
temporary emotional occupancy
```

The image should feel like someone was passing through, not posing for a final image.

---

# Review Questions

- Does the environment feel temporary?
- Does the image feel like arrival, exit, waiting, or drift?
- Did spatial incompleteness improve realism?
- Did the environment feel emotionally under-occupied?
- Would a phone have made the space too legible?
