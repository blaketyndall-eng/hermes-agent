# TRACE Environment-Aware Adaptation

TRACE should eventually adapt to the environment being photographed.

Not by forcing a style.
Not by applying a preset.

But by preserving the visual behavior that belongs to that environment.

---

# Core Principle

The scene should remain primary.

TRACE should respond to:

- light source type
- darkness level
- foreground/background relationship
- reflective surfaces
- movement likelihood
- emotional density

---

# Environment Examples

## Late sidewalk walk

Likely behaviors:

- deep shadow
- distant highlights
- uneven falloff
- slight motion softness
- environmental ambiguity

## Apartment kitchen

Likely behaviors:

- warm soft falloff
- muddy corners
- surface physicality
- low motion softness
- subdued memory pressure

## Parking lot flash

Likely behaviors:

- foreground pressure
- background collapse
- isolated highlights
- strong anti-phone potential
- sharp emotional immediacy

## Convenience store

Likely behaviors:

- fluorescent harshness
- reflective packaging
- cold visibility
- environmental emptiness
- over-clean risk

---

# What Environment Adaptation Should Do

It should gently influence:

- darkness confidence
- shadow texture
- falloff
- motion softness
- foreground pressure
- memory anchor weighting

---

# What It Should Avoid

Avoid:

- aesthetic scene presets
- fake cinematic looks
- forcing all images into one visual identity
- destroying scene-specific truth

---

# Long-Term Goal

TRACE should become better at preserving how different ordinary environments are emotionally remembered.

Not how they are maximally visible.
