# TRACE Failure Log

This document tracks failed behaviors and undesirable tendencies.

Failures are important.

The goal is not only to discover what works.
The goal is also to understand what breaks believability.

---

# Common Failure Modes

## Over-clean shadows

Problem:

Shadows become flat, lifted, and computational.

Result:

- atmosphere disappears
- physicality disappears
- image feels phone-processed

---

## Muddy blacks

Problem:

Dark regions lose dimensionality.

Result:

- clothing collapses
- surfaces merge together
- image feels digitally crushed

---

## Obvious texture

Problem:

Texture becomes visible as an effect.

Result:

- fake grain feeling
- overlay appearance
- aesthetic gimmick behavior

---

## Excessive contrast

Problem:

Contrast becomes stylized instead of believable.

Result:

- image feels preset-driven
- emotional realism decreases
- subtlety disappears

---

## Fake nostalgia behavior

Problem:

Image processing becomes obviously retro or cinematic.

Result:

- gimmick feeling
- weak emotional realism
- imitation instead of observation

---

## Over-stable images

Problem:

Everything appears equally clear and controlled.

Result:

- image feels computational
- no visual tension
- weak memory pressure

---

# Key Principle

TRACE should not:

- aggressively destroy images
- aggressively clean images

The strongest behavior is likely:

restrained instability.
