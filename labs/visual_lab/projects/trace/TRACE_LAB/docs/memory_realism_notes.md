# Memory Realism Notes

TRACE is not trying to maximize image clarity.

TRACE is trying to study what makes images feel remembered instead of merely documented.

---

# Key Idea

Human memory is incomplete.

We do not retain scenes with:

- perfect sharpness
- perfectly lifted shadows
- uniform clarity
- complete visibility

Instead, memory often preserves:

- mood
- light direction
- emotional pressure
- silhouettes
- darkness
- proximity
- instability
- environmental feeling

---

# Important Behaviors

## Darkness retention

Modern phones often remove darkness aggressively.

TRACE should become comfortable preserving partial visibility.

---

## Environmental ambiguity

Conflicting light temperatures and unstable color often feel emotionally believable.

TRACE should avoid over-correcting these behaviors.

---

## Subtle instability

Slight softness, shadow texture, and incomplete detail can increase emotional realism.

The key is restraint.

---

## Emotional pressure

Some images feel psychologically heavier because of:

- proximity
- flash collapse
- darkness
- visual uncertainty
- environmental isolation

This is not purely content-driven.

Visual behavior contributes heavily.

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should avoid:

- spectacle
- loud stylization
- fake nostalgia
- obvious effects

The strongest TRACE image may:

- feel emotionally distinct
- while remaining technically difficult to identify.
