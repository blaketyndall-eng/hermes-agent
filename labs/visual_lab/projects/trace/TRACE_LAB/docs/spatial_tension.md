# TRACE Spatial Tension

TRACE studies how imperfect visibility changes spatial feeling.

Especially under:

- direct flash
- darkness
- environmental collapse
- foreground isolation
- uneven exposure

---

# Core Principle

Many emotionally believable images feel:

```text
spatially incomplete
```

Examples:

- swallowed rooms
- detached foregrounds
- black corners
- floating faces
- collapsed backgrounds
- isolated highlights

Phones aggressively normalize this.

TRACE should increasingly preserve it.

---

# Current Spatial Signals

TRACE currently studies:

```text
foreground_dominance
background_retention
spatial_isolation
edge_darkness
```

These are:

- lightweight
- inspectable
- behavior-oriented

Avoid:

- depth ML
- semantic segmentation
- cinematic depth estimation

---

# 1. Foreground Dominance

Measures how visually dominant the image center is relative to edges.

Potential influence:

- foreground pressure
- flash isolation
- anti-phone behavior

---

# 2. Background Retention

Measures how much detail survives in dark regions.

Potential influence:

- dimensional black handling
- environmental persistence
- memory realism

---

# 3. Spatial Isolation

Measures separation between bright foreground and collapsed background.

Potential influence:

- emotional tension
- direct-flash realism
- spatial incompleteness

---

# 4. Edge Darkness

Measures darkness pressure around frame edges.

Potential influence:

- environmental collapse
- spatial ambiguity
- handheld realism

---

# Important Principle

TRACE should not maximize visibility.

It should preserve:

```text
believable incomplete space
```

---

# Long-Term Direction

Eventually:

```text
spatial signals
→ primitive adaptation
→ sequence spatial continuity
→ emotional realism
```

But the system should remain subtle and behavior-oriented.
