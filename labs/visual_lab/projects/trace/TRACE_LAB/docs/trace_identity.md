# TRACE Identity Notes

TRACE should not behave like a filter.

The long-term goal is subtle behavioral consistency.

---

# What TRACE should preserve

TRACE exists to preserve visual behaviors often erased by smartphone imaging pipelines.

Important behaviors:

- darkness
- falloff
- texture
- mixed lighting
- slight blur
- dimensional shadow
- imperfect clarity
- emotional ambiguity

---

# What TRACE should avoid

Avoid:

- obvious vintage aesthetics
- fake nostalgia
- heavy grain
- stylized destruction
- dramatic color shifts
- social-media preset behavior

The processing should feel difficult to identify directly.

---

# TRACE should feel like:

- remembered moments
- accidental photos
- emotionally ordinary scenes
- physical environments
- low-light memory
- unstable but believable capture

---

# The danger of over-stylization

If users immediately recognize:

"This is a TRACE filter"

then the system has likely become too aggressive.

The best TRACE behavior may be:

"I do not fully understand why this image feels different from phone photos."

---

# Long-term camera identity

TRACE identity should emerge from:

- repeated subtle tendencies
- stable exposure behavior
- believable shadow handling
- coherent roll behavior
- restrained texture

Not from loud visual gimmicks.

---

# Future research areas

Potential future areas:

- edge falloff
- low-light instability
- compression realism
- screenshot circulation
- flash collapse
- sensor behavior
- roll coherence
- accidental blur
- emotional image retention
